    Your Questions

    I tell people I live in a town so small we do not have a town drunk, so we all take turns. My town (population ~1500) has no sidewalks. No traffic lights. No Starbucks. One small convenience store. Deer, elk, and wild turkeys are spotted wandering through town. Sometimes cattle too. I walk to the end of my block and am in the forest. I have a trout stream within walking distance, and a couple more a short mountain bike ride away. I can paddle on a 7 mile long lake a five minute drive away, and be the only person in sight, other than the deer on the shore and the thousands of waterfowl sharing the lake with me. I can jump on my mountain bike and ride trails and forest roads all day without seeing another soul. A half hour drive puts me at a Wilderness Area trailhead where again I can hike and not see anyone. A traffic jam is two cars passing each other on the same street. Violent crime is virtually nonexistent (sometimes two drunks will get in a fight at the bar) and property crimes are low, mostly kids trying to steal something, and promptly caught. No gangs. The county seat is a half hour drive, with several grocery stores and a Walmart. The county seat has theaters, concerts, a symphony orchestra, plays, etc (my town has none of those). The downside is major shopping is 2 hours away in Reno or Chico. Winter storms sometimes make travel unsafe, and occasionally shuts off town from the outside world for a day or so. Power outages are common. Housing is cheap compared to the rest of California. You can buy a small fixer upper house for $30k, or a nice large one for $150K.
